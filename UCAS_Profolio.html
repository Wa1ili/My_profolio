All essays written by me


I have completed the Manchester Access Program provided by the University of Manchester. The project is a research-based essay with referencing to the materials assessed. The topic that I picked is 'Discuss the applications and the benefits of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the energy sector'. In this research task, I have learned more about the application of robotics, computer vision, machine learning, and natural language processing in the energy field; how to reference and find reliable resources online; how to write in formal languages, etc.
This project is quite challenging for me as it is my first time writing an essay, and have to distinguish enormous information. I have become more discreet when doing independent research since then.


This following essay was done alongside with an one-week work placement with Sutcliffe ( a structural and civil engineering firm in Liverpool,UK), about 'The Investigation of the design for a building and the environmental effects'.
In this project, I've learned the basic structure of a building and the carbon effects of the selection of material for construction. Also, I've gained insight into what it's like to work in an office and in the engineering industry.

The last essay is about a famous physical observation 'Quantum Tunelling'.
In this task, I attempted to explain this abstract idea with simple wordings that everyone could understand and tried to give analogies by other irrelevant theories that demonstrate the visuals of quantum tunelling effect. In contrast to the previous essays, this one requires more rigorous logic and precise details, so information from various online resources must be compared.
